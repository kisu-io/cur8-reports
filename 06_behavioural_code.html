Event-Type Overrides

🎵 Acoustic / Unplugged

  • Conversation noise must stop during performances — silence protocol
  • Phone on silent
  • One person leaves during set = one person returns after
  • Tipping bucket passed at set break only

🎧 DJ / Electronic Night

  • Dance floor zone opens (standing permitted)
  • No glass on dance floor — drinks decanted to plastic
  • No flash photography
  • No entry after 23:00

� privately booked Event

  • House rules communicated at entry — staff verbal + signage
  • Private event = venue privatised — no casual walk-ins
  • External promoter to supply behavioural briefing pre-event
  • Security ratio: 1 per 50 pax minimum

Module 3 — Practical Scenario Cards
S3-1 Customer on laptop, no headphones, volume too loud
1
Approach casually — "Hey, just a heads-up — headphones needed past this volume, for the space"
2
If no compliance → offer a headphone if spares available
3
If still non-compliant → "I need to ask you to lower it or use headphones — fair?"
4
If still resistance → "Happy to help find a spot where it's less disruptive" → offer alternative seating
5
Escalate to manager if still no resolution
S3-2 Customer attempting to bring outside alcohol in
1
Intercept at door — "Hey! Just a heads-up — outside alcohol isn't permitted here. We'll happy to pour you something from our menu"
2
If bag/cooler → don't touch, point to bag storage policy
3
If becomes defensive → "I totally get it, but it's our licence condition. Happy to keep it behind the bar till you leave, or you can enjoy it outside on the terrace"
4
Escalate only if aggression
S3-3 Customer filming during live set
1
Approach between songs — "No flash photography is fine, but just a heads-up — professional filming isn't permitted without prior booking. Happy to take a pic for you at the break!"
2
Offer to take photo on their phone
3
If professional equipment (DSLR/camcorder) → "This is a content restriction from the artist agreement"
4
No confrontation — document incident for manager
S3-4 Customer staying past 2-hour morning limit
1
At 1h45m → pass by: "All good here? Just a heads-up — we're peak morning and have a few waiting. Happy to let you stay a bit longer if things quiet down"
2
At 2h → approach: "Hey — just so you know, we've hit our 2-hour window. No pressure, but we're at capacity. I can put your name down for when a spot opens?"
3
Offer alternatives: terrace, bar seating, takeaway
4
Never forcibly remove — escalate to manager
Module 4 — Performance Night Protocols
Door Protocol — Performance Night
  • ID check: every patron, every time — no exceptions
  • Stamp/wristband: 18+ verification
  • Search policy: bags visible, no glass, no external alcohol
  • Capacity count — maintain room count
  • Re-entry: NO re-entry after door close
  • Artist brief: confirm no restricting items (confetti, open flame, etc.)
Bar Protocol — Performance Night
  • Order queue: single queue, no approaching bar during live sets
  • Staff call "Next please" — no ad-hoc orders during sets
  • Drink tickets: optional for busy nights
  • Last call: 30 min before close — visually communicated + verbal
  • Post-last-call: no new orders after called
  • Glass policy: plastic cups for floor, glass behind bar only
Floor Protocol — Performance Night
  • Zone: front-of-stage area no standing during acts
  • Safety: clear exit paths, no bags blocking egress
  • Monitoring: 1 staff eyes on floor at all times during live sets
  • Intervention: any fall, spill, or aggression → signal bar immediately
  • Photos: allow phone, discourage flash, no obstruction of sight lines
Module 5 — Conflict De-escalation Ladder
1
Ambient Approach
Eye contact, smile, open body language. Intercept before it becomes a problem.
2
Verbal Redirect
Offer alternative. Use customer's name if known. Stay calm.
3
Firm Boundary
State the rule + consequence once. Do NOT argue — state, pause, wait.
4
Exit Request
"I'm going to ask you to leave now. I'm not going to debate it further."
5
Physical Threat
Press SOS. Do NOT physically intervene alone. Manager + authorities if required.
Key Phrases — Practise These
Over capacity"We're at capacity right now — I can put you on the list and text you when a spot opens"
Volume complaint"Volume is set by the artist for this set. Happy to grab you earplugs if it's too much?"
Rules complaint"The rules aren't personal — they're here so everyone enjoys the night. I can explain the thinking"
Refusing to leave"I'm not going to argue about this. You need to leave — I'll help you get a taxi/another venue"
Drugs suspicion"Zero tolerance here. I'd like you to show me what's in your hand"
Module 6 — Event-Type Delivery Checklists
Acoustic Night Delivery
  • Tables arranged for listening (no centre tables blocking sight lines)
  • Lighting: low, warm — no overhead fluorescents
  • Sound: check monitor levels, ensure performers can hear themselves
  • Set list posted or communicated to floor staff
  • No food service during sets (kitchen quiet protocol)
  • Tip bucket: passed at set break only
  • Phone policy: staff reminder to announce "phones on silent"
DJ Night Delivery
  • Dance floor zone marked (stanchions or floor tape)
  • Plastic cups: pre-staged near dance floor
  • Glass policy enforced — no glass past bar counter edge
  • Capacity: tighter monitoring, 1 staff on door at all times
  • Bouncer/Security: confirmed, on-site 30 min before doors
  • Water station: free water near dance floor
  • Last entry time communicated + enforced
Private Event Delivery
  • Pre-event briefing with host/organiser
  • Custom house rules agreed and printed for door staff
  • Capacity agreed — do not exceed
  • Payment / tab arrangement confirmed
  • External promoter insurance/liability confirmed
  • Staff assigned: 1 manager, floor coverage ratio
  • Cleanup + security deposit agreed post-event

Emergency Protocol

  1. ASSESS — Is anyone in immediate danger?
  2. ALERT — Signal bar/staff without causing panic
  3. CALL — Emergency services if needed
  4. CONTAIN — Secure the area, don't move injured persons
  5. GUIDE — Direct emergency services on arrival
  6. DOCUMENT — Incident report within 24 hours
